History of The Internet

The internet its the connection between the whole world. It works like a net or a spider web.

It began with networks that were designed as a way of remotely share program files and access other computers.

Lately evolved to a distributed communication system, divided in blocks that has a fast connection between them.

Around the 60's the internet was an idea of a military connective system that could withstand over a nuclear blast ,

that reason they decide to out spread the points of connectivity between the appliances in such a way that formed as we know as a WEB or NET.

Instead of having all system in one unique point, the information between theses points had to be passed fast and unchangeable.

The internet is owned by the service providers, they are who you pay to make sure that the information you send is going to the right direction, end to end.

The way it works behind your screen is rather complex but to simplify its like you make an order on a restaurant.

  1. Call the waiter.
  2. Choose what you want.
  3. Place your order.
  4. Dish is made by the chef.
  5. The waiter bring it back to you.
  6. Pay the bill.
The Internet works basicaly the same way!
  1. Choose what you want.
  2. Click in a link to make a request.
  3. Your request is send to a host or server.
  4. That will processed.
  5. Redirect you to the link requested.
  6. Pay the bill once a month for the service provided.

Note in that simple formula you would be able to connect in to a single source, but as we know they evolved and adapt

now you are able to connect in many sources of information at same time due a system that they called package system

where many sources send small packages of information that is build up on the go.

This system have a standard formatting protocol to make sure all computer connected is speaking the same language

this system is well know as TCP/IP.

TCP/IP stands for Transmission Control Protocol/ Internet Protocol.

At the start they had a unique place to store all data between computer and it was located at Stanford in USA

but with the growth of the internet and it spread around the country other hosts started to join in to hold up that data

that couldn't be store in Stanford any longer , due the amount of space required.

So they decide to create a new system that is called DNS or Domain Name System

this system is what manage every host and their information in to different departments

it was like a library of addresses and DNS was a way to catalogue everything and send the correct address upon requested

in this way they could keep track of all domains and its layers.

Was only about 1991 when the internet was accessible by the general public

until this point was first a military communication system as I mentioned and extended to university level starting being a communication between Stanford and UCLA

lately other university started to join and a better system had to be implemented.

The military product evolved to a more broad audience at this point the military decided to move on to other projects

since that was accomplished and other company (NSFNET) took over the project making the internet accessible to the public at a price.
